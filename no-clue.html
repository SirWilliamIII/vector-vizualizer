start here if you’re lost

You have no idea what this is. That’s fine.

Read this one page. After that, you’ll still be confused, but at least you’ll be confused in the right direction.

step 1: the dots are your words

Every dot you see is a word or short phrase you (or the app) added.

The closer two dots are, the more this little math brain thinks they “go together.” Far apart = “eh, not really related.”

That’s it. No magic. Dots = words. Distance = “how similar” in math land.

step 2: the computer doesn’t read words, it reads numbers

The computer does not “understand” English. It turns each word into a long list of numbers.

I’ll use one grown-up word once: vector. Here it just means “a list of numbers.” Nothing deeper.

example

"dog" → [0.12, -0.87, 0.44, …] "cat" → [0.10, -0.82, 0.47, …]

The lists are close, so the dots end up close.

step 3: the space moves because math is rude

When you add a new word, the app doesn’t just drop one new dot in. It re-thinks where all the dots should go.

  • You add a word.
  • The math brain gives it a list of numbers.
  • The app re-shuffles the whole map to fit everything together.
  • All dots may move a bit. That’s on purpose.

So no, your eyes aren’t broken. The map really is changing under you.

step 4: there are 3 tiny brains arguing

Up top, you can pick which “model” to use. Think of them as three tiny, stubborn brains.

  • Brain A: “These two sentences sound the same.”
  • Brain B: “This looks like something you’d search on the internet.”
  • Brain C: “This feels like a thing you’d want to retrieve.”

Same words, different brain → the dots often move to new spots.

If you switch the model and everything shifts? Good. You just saw that “meaning” is not one fixed thing.

step 5: clicking two dots is where the fun is

When you click two dots, the app stops being polite and starts being specific.

  • It tells you how close they are on this map.
  • It shows “how similar” they are as a simple number.
  • It draws lines and shapes to show the gap between them.
plain version

Big number = model thinks they’re very related. Medium number = “eh, kind of.” Tiny or negative number = “nope, not friends.”

step 6: zooming around is not just for looks

When you drag, zoom, and spin the scene, you’re not just playing with the camera. You’re training your brain to see patterns this math brain already sees.

  • Clusters of dots = topics.
  • Lonely dots = weird outliers.
  • Bridges between groups = interesting “in between” ideas.

If you notice something and think “huh, that’s odd,” congrats. That’s the whole point.

step 7: batch upload is chaos mode

When you upload a bunch of text at once, the app:

  • Splits it into chunks.
  • Turns each chunk into that “list of numbers” idea.
  • Rebuilds the whole map from scratch.

This is where things really move. If it feels like you shook a snow globe, that’s about right.

what you know now (that you didn’t two minutes ago)

You now know:

  • Each dot is just your text turned into numbers.
  • Distance = “how similar” in this model’s opinion.
  • Adding and deleting words moves the whole space, not just one point.
  • Different models → different layouts of the same words.
  • Clicking two dots lets you see exactly how close or far they are.

You still don’t “understand AI.” No one does. But if you say: “it’s just turning text into numbers and plotting them”, you’re already less wrong than most people.
